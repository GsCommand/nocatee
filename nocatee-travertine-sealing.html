Natural stone care

Travertine needs a process suited to porous stone.

Nocatee outdoor travertine can show embedded soil, organic shading, mineral residue, and remnants of earlier treatments. We first distinguish routine buildup from etching, chips, and permanent variation so expectations are clear before cleaning begins.

Surface-appropriate preparation and careful moisture checks help protect the character of the stone. Sealer selection is based on the location, exposure, and desired appearance rather than treating travertine like ordinary concrete pavers.

  1. Assess: identify stone condition, staining, repairs, and old coatings.
  2. Clean: use an approach appropriate for natural stone.
  3. Dry: allow moisture to leave the porous surface.
  4. Protect: apply the agreed sealer evenly and review the finish.
