Local conditions

Nocatee driveways fail for reasons the rest of Jacksonville doesn’t share

Nocatee driveway paver sealing requires more than a quick wash and a coat of sealer. Reclaimed-water rust staining, aging builder-applied coatings, intense sun and storm-season joint washout can all affect how a driveway should be cleaned, re-sanded and sealed.

HydroSeal evaluates the existing surface first so paver cleaning and sealing in Nocatee is matched to the driveway’s coating, drainage, stains and joint condition.

Reclaimed irrigation water

Iron and minerals in irrigation overspray can leave orange or brown staining. Rust should be treated before sealing: sealing over untreated rust staining can trap or emphasize the discoloration and make correction more difficult. Treatment improves many stains, but complete removal depends on their age, depth and the paver material.

Builder-grade sealer reaching end of life

Some builder-applied sealers may begin showing wear, patchiness, whitening or flaking after several years, depending on the product, preparation, traffic and exposure. A failed paver sealer may need targeted removal or full stripping before paver resealing in Nocatee.

Storm-season joint washout

Heavy rain, roof runoff and routine cleaning can lower joint-sand levels. Restoring appropriate joint depth helps support interlock and gives joint-stabilizing sealer material to bind. Drainage or movement issues may require repair beyond adding sand.

  1. Inspect
    Identify the paver material, existing coating, joint loss, drainage, repairs and stains.
  2. Treat
    Address rust, organic growth, efflorescence or failed coatings with the treatment appropriate to the surface.
  3. Clean
    Remove soil and buildup while managing pressure around joints, edges and adjacent surfaces.
  4. Re-sand
    Replace missing joint sand to the appropriate level after cleaning and needed repairs.
  5. Dry and verify
    Allow the surface and joints to dry, then confirm weather and moisture conditions before sealing.
  6. Seal
    Apply the selected breathable sealer at a rate suited to the paver and project conditions.

Joint sand

The joints decide how long the finish lasts

Cleaning without replacing washed-out joint sand leaves paver edges unsupported and the finished surface incomplete. HydroSeal generally uses ASTM C144 kiln-dried joint sand with a joint-stabilizing sealer. The appropriate joint material depends on the paver system, drainage, existing materials and project conditions.

Joint stabilization can help reduce sand loss and surface movement, but it does not repair a failed base or eliminate future upkeep. Treatment and stabilized joints may reduce recurring weed and ant activity, but results vary and future maintenance may still be needed.

Timing

When you can use the driveway again

Cure windows depend on sealer chemistry, temperature, humidity, shade and rain. Follow the project-specific instructions HydroSeal provides after application.

  • Vehicles: keep vehicles off for at least 48 hours unless instructed otherwise.
  • Sprinklers and water: keep the surface dry through the stated cure window and adjust irrigation before service.

Traction depends on the paver texture, sealer, application rate, moisture, contaminants and maintenance. Sealed surfaces may become slippery when wet.
