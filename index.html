Local conditions

Why Nocatee pavers need a surface-specific plan

Reclaimed irrigation staining. Nocatee driveways, walkways and pool-deck pavers are frequently exposed to reclaimed irrigation overspray. Minerals and iron in the water can leave orange, brown or rust-colored arcs across concrete and brick pavers. Standard pressure washing may lighten surface dirt without fully correcting bonded irrigation staining, so these areas should be identified and treated before paver sealing. Applying sealer over untreated discoloration can make the staining more noticeable or make later correction more difficult.

Existing and builder-applied coatings. Older or builder-applied paver sealers may develop whitening, cloudiness, patchy sheen, flaking or uneven wear. Before completing Nocatee paver cleaning and sealing, HydroSeal evaluates the existing coating, moisture conditions, surface preparation and compatibility with the proposed sealer. Some surfaces may be suitable for cleaning and resealing, while failed coatings may require additional preparation or stripping before a new system is applied.

Joint-sand loss and paver movement. Heavy Florida rain, roof runoff, irrigation, pressure cleaning and normal traffic can lower the sand between driveway and patio pavers. Proper joint levels help support paver interlock and improve the finished appearance after sealing. Re-sanding can restore depleted joints, but it does not repair a failed base, severe settlement or drainage problem. Sunken, rocking or separated pavers should be evaluated and repaired before the sealing process begins.

Screened pool decks and shaded areas. Nocatee pool-deck pavers and travertine inside screened enclosures often receive less direct sunlight and airflow than an open driveway. Shade, humidity and repeated water exposure can slow drying and encourage algae, mildew and organic buildup. Pool deck paver sealing therefore requires careful cleaning, adequate drying time and a breathable sealer selected for the surface material and wet-area conditions.

Different materials require different preparation. Concrete pavers, brick pavers, travertine and natural stone should not automatically receive the same cleaning chemicals, pressure or sealer. Travertine sealing in Nocatee requires stone-compatible cleaning and attention to open fill, moisture and surface finish. Driveway paver sealing must account for vehicle traffic, irrigation staining and tire marks, while pool deck sealing places additional emphasis on drainage, wet conditions and traction.

Florida sun, heat and moisture. Full-sun driveways can experience stronger ultraviolet exposure and surface heating, while shaded patios may retain moisture longer after cleaning or rain. These differences affect drying, sealer absorption, application timing and long-term appearance. HydroSeal bases the paver sealing plan on the actual material, existing coating, stain conditions, drainage, sun exposure and intended use of each surface.

Process

How we clean, re-sand and seal pavers

  1. Inspect: material, coating, joints, drainage, stains and repairs.
  2. Pre-treat: target organic growth, rust, oil or efflorescence where appropriate.
  3. Clean: remove buildup with pressure and chemistry suited to the surface.
  4. Repair: complete agreed releveling or replacement before sealing.
  5. Re-sand: restore joint levels with the appropriate material.
  6. Dry and verify: confirm the surface and weather are ready.
  7. Seal: apply the selected breathable sealer at the proper rate.
  8. Walk through: review cure time and care instructions.

HydroSeal generally uses ASTM C144 kiln-dried joint sand with a compatible joint-stabilizing sealer. The right joint material depends on the paver system, drainage, existing materials and project conditions.

How often should pavers be sealed?

Resealing timing depends on the previous product, traffic, sun, water exposure, maintenance and surface condition. Many surfaces are evaluated every few years, but the correct timing should be based on the actual condition of the pavers.

Why is my Nocatee driveway turning orange?

Orange or brown arcs are often mineral or iron staining from reclaimed irrigation overspray. Pressure washing alone may not remove it, so the surface should be evaluated for targeted treatment before sealing.

Do you seal travertine and pool decks?

Yes. HydroSeal seals paver pool decks, travertine and natural stone using surface-appropriate cleaning methods and breathable sealer selected for the material and site conditions.

Will sealing make my pavers slippery?

Any wet paver or stone surface can become slippery. Traction depends on surface texture, sealer, application rate, finish, contaminants and maintenance, especially around pools.

How long before I can park on the driveway?
